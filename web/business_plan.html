04 · Competitive Analysis
Verity's Defensible Moat
Fiserv agentOS
AI-enhanced legacy platform. AWS Bedrock AgentCore integration. Launched May 2026, full availability August 2026. Focus: internal bank workflow automation. No formal verification, no sovereign single-binary deployment, no capability-based microkernel. Verity advantage: agentOS is an AI orchestration layer on legacy infrastructure; Verity's 1A1A framework is built into the kernel.
Thought Machine Vault Core
Smart-contract banking core. Strong composability. Trusted by Tier 1 banks including JPMorgan and Lloyds. No TLA+ verification, no agent-native identity, no concurrent multi-TEE trust layer. SaaS subscription pricing. Verity advantage: Formal verification, air-gap deployment, agent-native architecture.
Mambu
Cloud-based SaaS core banking for fintechs and banks. Claims 35–50% cost reduction vs. legacy. Freemium entry point. No formal verification, no agent-native features, no air-gap support. Verity advantage: Mambu targets the low-to-mid market; Verity targets the high-assurance sovereign tier.
Temenos
Average annual contract ~$518K. Mid-market implementations: $8–15M initial licensing + $2–4M annual maintenance. Implementation: 18–36 months. Verity advantage: Verity's perpetual license at $600K–$2.8M delivers significantly lower TCO.
Anchorage Digital
Federally chartered crypto bank. Launched "Agentic Banking" May 2026 with Google Cloud. 20 financial institutions queued for stablecoin issuance. Crypto-first. Verity advantage: Anchorage is crypto-first; Verity is banking-first with optional tokenized deposit capabilities. Anchorage requires Google Cloud; Verity deploys anywhere.
10x Banking
Cloud-native "meta core" platform. Volume-based SaaS pricing. No formal verification, no agent-native features. Verity advantage: Formal verification, sovereign deployment, broader service domain coverage (328 BIAN domains).
